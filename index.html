Introduction to Therians

Therians are individuals who identify as non-human animals on a spiritual or psychological level. This identity is often deeply personal and intrinsic to the individual.

About Therians

The term 'therian' comes from the word 'therianthropy', which refers to the mythological ability of humans to metamorphose into animals. Modern therians, however, do not claim to physically transform but rather experience a shift in mindset or spirit that aligns with an animal identity.

The Journey of Self-Discovery

For many therians, the journey of self-discovery begins in childhood. They might feel a strong connection to a particular animal, experiencing what is known as a 'shifting'. This can manifest in various ways, from dreams to a deep sense of empathy with the animal.

Understanding and accepting this identity can be a challenging yet rewarding experience. It involves introspection, community support, and often a lot of research into both the animal kingdom and one's own psyche.
